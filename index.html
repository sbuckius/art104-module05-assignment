Part 4 · Speculative Artwork Idea

100 pts · Group
Due March 26, 2026 @ 11 am
Instructions & Resources Speculative Artwork Overview (Part 4) — click to expand

Create a speculative artwork idea that is related to Nature/Ecology/Environment/Biology + your theme. See Speculative Everything (PDF) for conceptual approaches. Ideas for inspiration: Acute Art — Discover.

Possible Conceptual Approaches:

  • Nature/Ecology + Your Theme Design: Design your own Nature/Ecology + Your Theme machine/device/artwork that reflects concepts, inspirations, critiques of ideas at the intersection between your theme and Nature/Ecology. What would it look like? Utopian or dystopian slant?
  • Fictional Worlds: Imagine a world that includes a device/technology of your creation. What is this world like? What does your device do? How does it work? What code would it need? Can you create an image, video game scene, or net art piece for this fictional world?
  • Utopia/Dystopia: Present a utopian or dystopian view. Create an image for a scene from a video game or a net-art piece as the portal to this world.
  • Idea As Story: Create a speculative artwork about a device/technology that exists in a story. What if it's set 50 million years in the future?
  • What if?: What if your device/technology went rogue? Became your best friend? Could draw? What does it look like? How does it work?
  • Video Game Design: Make a video game that involves imagined devices/technologies from your theme. What would they do, look like, how would they work? What would the setting be?
  • Thought Experiments: What if your theme's technology were invented 1000 years ago? What if it made your work slower? What if it were implanted in your brain? What if it were designed only for a certain type of person?
  • Reductio Ad Absurdum: Think of a claim about a technology/concept in your theme. Take it to the extreme — the result would be absurd, proving the original claim incorrect. How could you show this in an image, drawing, code, or video game?
  • CounterFactual: Change a historical fact related to your theme to see what would happen if… What would the outcome be? More on counterfactual thinking.
  • Tweaking the Everyday: Make a slight change to a technology of today to address a concern, idea, or experience you have with it.
  • Constructed Realities & Unrealities: Construct a new reality for your theme — possible or impossible.
  • Imaginary Institution: Design a new company, organization, or institution related to your theme. What code would they employ? A website, video game, drawing, or video?
  • Chain Reactions: Start with one idea. What is the chain reaction for the future if things evolve from this design?

Possible Depiction Mediums (a sketch is completely fine!):

  • Hand sketches on paper — example 1, Abu Bakarr Mansaray, Amy Cutler
  • Drawings/sketches in any digital program
  • Illustrator 3D sketches or Blender Render — Dunne & Raby example, Lawrence Lek
  • Digital Photography + Blender Render — example
  • Drawing with p5.js or other code — p5.js example, OpenProcessing example
  • Digital diagram — Anatomy of AI
  • Dunne & Raby — Designs for an Overpopulated Planet
  • Daisy Ginsberg — Growth Assembly
  • Paul Noble — A Cartography of the Self
  • Whitney Museum — Example work
  • Codex Seraphinianus (Luigi Serafini)
  • Petra Cortright, Danziger Gallery
  • SF Arts Commission — Public Art Proposals
