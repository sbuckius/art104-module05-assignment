Our Technology Is
Bioinformatics & Genomic Sequencing
Computational tools that read, map, and interpret DNA — turning the code of living organisms into data that can be visualized, compared, and creatively repurposed.
▶ Eduardo Kac — Genesis▸ Wikipedia
Environmental Sensors & IoT
Networks of physical devices — air quality monitors, soil probes, water sensors — that collect real-time ecological data and connect the living environment to digital systems.
▶ Jeremijenko TED▸ Wikipedia
Satellite Imaging & Remote Sensing
Orbital and aerial technologies that capture the Earth's surface across wavelengths invisible to the human eye, revealing deforestation, glacial retreat, and ecosystem change from above.
▶ Google Earth Timelapse▸ Wikipedia
CRISPR & Genetic Engineering
Precision tools for rewriting the genetic instructions of living organisms — raising urgent questions about who controls life, what counts as natural, and where biology ends and design begins.
▶ Kurzgesagt — CRISPR▸ Wikipedia
Ecological Simulation & Modeling
Computational models that replicate the behavior of ecosystems, populations, and climate systems — allowing scientists and artists to run, observe, and intervene in virtual natural worlds.
▶ Karl Sims — Evolved Creatures▸ Wikipedia
Bio-inspired Design & Biomimicry
Design and engineering strategies that borrow from natural forms, processes, and systems — reimagining technology through the logic of evolution.
▶ Benyus TED▸ Wikipedia
Drone & Aerial Surveillance
Unmanned aerial vehicles used to monitor wildlife, map terrain, and document environmental change — tools that extend human vision into remote or inaccessible ecosystems.
▶ Tega Brain — Asunder▸ Wikipedia
Wearable Biosensors
Body-worn devices that measure physiological signals — heartbeat, sweat, breath, movement — blurring the boundary between organism and instrument, self and data.
▸ Wikipedia
AI & Machine Learning for Ecology
Algorithms trained on ecological datasets to identify species, predict climate patterns, detect poaching, and model biodiversity.
▶ Refik Anadol — Large Nature Model▸ Wikipedia
Synthetic Biology
Engineering entirely new biological parts, organisms, and systems that do not exist in nature — programming living cells like software.
▶ Tissue Culture & Art Project▸ Wikipedia
Microscopy & Imaging Technologies
Tools that make the invisible visible — electron microscopes, fluorescence imaging, and macro photography that reveal the architecture of cells and organisms.
▸ Wikipedia
Citizen Science Platforms & Apps
Digital tools like iNaturalist and eBird that recruit everyday people to collect environmental data — democratizing scientific observation.
▶ Ken Goldberg — Telegarden▸ Wikipedia
Telepresence & Robotic Systems
Technologies that allow humans to act at a distance — robots tending remote gardens, cameras inhabiting animal bodies.
▶ Eduardo Kac — Rara Avis▸ Wikipedia
Virtual & Augmented Reality for Nature
Immersive technologies that simulate or overlay natural environments — from VR forests to AR tools that reveal hidden layers of the living world.
▶ Char Davies — Ephémère▸ Wikipedia

Presentation Template (Day 2): Open Template

Create a speculative artwork idea related to Nature/Ecology/Environment/Biology + your theme. Final image MUST be created by you, NOT AI.
🌐 Possible Approaches — from Speculative Everything by Dunne & Raby
Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme Design
Design your own Nature + Technology machine/device that reflects concepts, inspirations, critiques of ideas at the intersection between technology and Nature.
Fictional Worlds
Imagine a world that includes a Nature + Technology device/machine of your creation. What is this world like? What does your device do?
Utopia/Dystopia
Create a Nature + Technology device/machine that presents a utopian or dystopian view.
Idea As Story
Create an idea for a speculative artwork about a Nature + Technology device that exists in a story.
What if?
What if your Nature + Technology device went rogue? What if it became your best friend? What if it could draw?
Video Game Design
What if you made a video game that involved imagined Nature + Technology devices/machines?
Thought Experiments
What if your Nature + Technology device were invented 1000 years ago? What if it made your work slower instead of faster?
Reductio Ad Absurdum
Think of a claim about your device. Take it to the extreme — the result is absurd, which proves the original claim was incorrect.
CounterFactual
Change a historical fact about your technology to see what would happen if…
Tweaking the Everyday
Make a slight change in the Nature + Technology of today to address a concern, idea, opinion, or experience.
Constructed Realities
Construct a new reality for Nature + Technology using your imagination — it could be possible or impossible.
Imaginary Institution
What if you designed a new Nature + Technology company, organization, or institution?
Chain Reactions
Start with one idea for a device. What is the chain reaction for the future if these devices evolve from this design?
