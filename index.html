Team Information

Activist, Critical & Dialogic Practices
Art that uses the logic of the internet against itself, critiques extraction and waste, or builds new social ecologies. Works that understand culture as co-producing environmental conditions.
▸ Tate: Activist Art▸ Dialogic▸ Critical Theory
Artificial Life
Code that evolves, simulates biological growth, and asks whether digital processes can constitute genuine life. Swarm intelligence, virtual creatures, organism-as-algorithm.
▸ Artificial Life▸ Swarm Intelligence▸ Evolutionary Algorithm
Biotech & Genetics
Artists at the intersection of biology and technology — encoding DNA, growing tissue sculptures, critiquing GMOs. Political works about who controls life itself.
▸ Biotechnology▸ Genetics▸ GMO
Telepresence & Remote Ecologies
Works that let you tend a garden or inhabit a bird's body from across the world via the internet — collapsing distance while introducing radical mediation between human and nature.
▸ Telepresence▸ Ecology▸ Telerobotics
Hidden Ecology of the Internet
Data centers, e-waste, rare earth minerals — the material and ecological cost of so-called "immaterial" digital media. Making the invisible visible.
▸ Environmental Impact of Computing▸ E-waste▸ Data Centers
Virtual Nature & Immersive Space
Can a digital landscape carry ecological meaning? VR environments that evoke fog, water, soil, and seasons — asking whether virtual nature can generate genuine ecological consciousness.
▸ Virtual Reality▸ Immersive Technology▸ Nature Connectedness
Interspecies & Post-human
Using technology to bridge human and nonhuman worlds — from plants as interface devices to embodying a bird online. Can empathy traverse species boundaries through digital mediation?
▸ Interspecies Communication▸ Posthumanism▸ Multispecies
Ecocriticism & Climate Media
Scholarly and artistic frameworks for understanding how media represents, communicates, and intervenes in ecological crisis. Data visualization, climate activism, environmental pedagogy.
▸ Ecocriticism▸ Climate Change Art▸ Environmental Communication

Our Technology Is
Bioinformatics & Genomic Sequencing
Computational tools that read, map, and interpret DNA — turning the code of living organisms into data that can be visualized, compared, and creatively repurposed.
▶ Eduardo Kac — Genesis▸ Wikipedia
Environmental Sensors & IoT
Networks of physical devices — air quality monitors, soil probes, water sensors — that collect real-time ecological data and connect the living environment to digital systems.
▶ Jeremijenko TED▸ Wikipedia
Satellite Imaging & Remote Sensing
Orbital and aerial technologies that capture the Earth's surface across wavelengths invisible to the human eye, revealing deforestation, glacial retreat, and ecosystem change from above.
▶ Google Earth Timelapse▸ Wikipedia
CRISPR & Genetic Engineering
Precision tools for rewriting the genetic instructions of living organisms — raising urgent questions about who controls life, what counts as natural, and where biology ends and design begins.
▶ Kurzgesagt — CRISPR▸ Wikipedia
Ecological Simulation & Modeling
Computational models that replicate the behavior of ecosystems, populations, and climate systems — allowing scientists and artists to run, observe, and intervene in virtual natural worlds.
▶ Karl Sims — Evolved Creatures▸ Wikipedia
Bio-inspired Design & Biomimicry
Design and engineering strategies that borrow from natural forms, processes, and systems — spider silk, termite ventilation, leaf photosynthesis — reimagining technology through the logic of evolution.
▶ Benyus TED▸ Wikipedia
Drone & Aerial Surveillance
Unmanned aerial vehicles used to monitor wildlife, map terrain, and document environmental change — tools that extend human vision into remote or inaccessible ecosystems.
▶ Tega Brain — Asunder▸ Wikipedia
Wearable Biosensors
Body-worn devices that measure physiological signals — heartbeat, sweat, breath, movement — blurring the boundary between organism and instrument, self and data.
▶ Interactive Plant Growing▸ Wikipedia
AI & Machine Learning for Ecology
Algorithms trained on ecological datasets to identify species, predict climate patterns, detect poaching, and model biodiversity — intelligence applied to the more-than-human world.
▶ Refik Anadol — Large Nature Model▸ Wikipedia
Synthetic Biology
Engineering entirely new biological parts, organisms, and systems that do not exist in nature — programming living cells like software to produce light, materials, or ecological functions.
▶ Tissue Culture & Art Project▸ Wikipedia
Microscopy & Imaging Technologies
Tools that make the invisible visible — electron microscopes, fluorescence imaging, and macro photography that reveal the architecture of cells, fungi, and organisms at scales beyond unaided perception.
▶ Sommerer & Mignonneau — A-Volve▸ Wikipedia
Citizen Science Platforms & Apps
Digital tools like iNaturalist and eBird that recruit everyday people to collect environmental data — democratizing scientific observation and turning public attention into ecological knowledge.
▶ Ken Goldberg — Telegarden▸ Wikipedia
Telepresence & Robotic Systems
Technologies that allow humans to act at a distance — robots tending remote gardens, cameras inhabiting animal bodies — mediating presence between humans and far-off or nonhuman environments.
▶ Eduardo Kac — Rara Avis▸ Wikipedia
Virtual & Augmented Reality for Nature
Immersive technologies that simulate or overlay natural environments — from VR forests that evoke embodied ecological experience to AR tools that reveal hidden layers of the living world.
▶ Char Davies — Ephémère▸ Wikipedia

Presentation Template (Day 2): Open Template

Create a speculative artwork idea related to Nature/Ecology/Environment/Biology + your theme. Final image MUST be created by you, NOT AI.
🌐 Possible Approaches — from Speculative Everything by Dunne & Raby
Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme Design
Design your own Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme machine/device that reflects concepts, inspirations, critiques of ideas at the intersection between technology and Nature. What would it look like? Maybe this design proposes utopian visions of a technological identity or critiques technological identities from today or the past in a more dystopian slant.
Fictional Worlds
Imagine a world that includes a Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme device/machine of your creation. What is this world like? What does your device do? What does it look like? How does it work? What code would it need to function? Can you think of an artwork based on this code? Can you think of an image that would depict this fictional world or represent a video game involving these devices? Maybe you create a net art piece — a series of hyperlinks — for your fictional Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme device?
Utopia/Dystopia
Create a Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme device/machine that presents a utopian or dystopian view. Maybe you create an image for a scene from a video game about this device. What if you made a net-art piece that is the portal to the device in your dystopian world?
Idea As Story
Create an idea for a speculative artwork about a Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme device that exists in a story. What if the story is set 50 million years in the future? How would the device work? What would its function be? What would it look like? What type of code would it need to run? What if you created an idea for a video game about this story? How can you show this in an image?
What if?
What if your Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme device went rogue? What if it became your best friend? What if it could draw? What does it look like? How does it work? What code does it run? What if it is part of a video game? How could you create an image that shows your idea? How would you create a net-art piece — a series of links — that reveals the rogue device to the user?
Video Game Design
What if you made a video game that involved imagined Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme devices/machines? What would they do? What would they look like? How would they work? What new functions would they have? What would the setting be — a lab? The jungle? A spaceship?
Thought Experiments
What if your Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme device were invented 1000 years ago — what would it be like now? What if it made your work slower instead of faster? What if it were implanted in your brain? What if it were designed only for a certain type of person? What if something in its history didn't happen the way it did? For your selected question: what does it do, how does it work, what does it look like, and how can you depict this idea through code, imagery, or a video game scene?
Reductio Ad Absurdum
"Reductio ad absurdum is a mode of argumentation that seeks to establish a contention by deriving an absurdity from its denial." Think of a claim about your Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme device. Take it to the extreme — the result is absurd, which proves the original claim was incorrect. How could you show this idea in an image, drawing, rendering, code, video game, or glitch image?
CounterFactual
Change a historical fact about Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme to see what would happen if… "Counterfactual thinking involves the human tendency to create possible alternatives to life events that have already occurred." Think of an event in your technology's history. What would happen if it occurred differently, or not at all? What would the outcome be for the device you imagine given this change?
Tweaking the Everyday
Make a slight change in the Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme of today to address a concern, idea, opinion, or experience you have with the technology. How would you show your idea?
Constructed Realities & Unrealities
Construct a new reality for Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme using your imagination — it could be possible or impossible. What would Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme be like in this new reality?
Imaginary Institution
What if you designed a new Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme company, organization, or institution? What would the devices be like in this new company? What would they be like in your imaginary non-profit? What code would they employ? How could you visualize this idea — a website, a video game, a drawing, a video?
Chain Reactions
Start with one idea for a Nature + Your Technology and/or Your Theme device. What is the chain reaction for the future if these devices evolve from this design? What do the new devices look like? How do they work? What does the world in which they operate look like?
